The Hidden Cost of Missing Deliveries

You've been there. Concrete truck arrives at 7 AM but no one's on site. Steel shipment shows up but the superintendent is at another job. Framing lumber gets delivered to the wrong address. Each missed delivery costs you $500-2,000 in standby fees, rescheduling, and delays.

SiteProc's construction delivery tracking software solves this. Every delivery is tracked, every arrival triggers alerts, and every receipt is photo-documented. No more "he said, she said" disputes about what was delivered and when.

SiteProc turns delivery chaos into delivery confidence in three steps:

  • Kanban board view for visual delivery management

Photo Proof of Delivery

No more disputes about what was delivered. SiteProc captures photo proof at delivery:

  • Timestamped photos with GPS location data
  • Condition documentation showing damage or issues
  • Quantity verification comparing delivered vs. ordered
  • Digital signature from receiver confirming receipt

Every photo is stored permanently and linked to the delivery record. When a supplier claims they delivered 500 sheets of drywall and you only received 400, you have proof.

Instant Alert System

Stay informed without constantly checking the app. SiteProc sends alerts when:

  • A delivery is marked "in transit" by the vendor
  • Delivery arrives at the job site
  • Delivery is marked complete with photo proof
  • A delivery is running late (30+ minutes past scheduled time)
  • Issues are flagged (damage, shortage, wrong materials)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do my field team members mark deliveries as received?

They use the SiteProc mobile web app (works on any phone browser). When a delivery arrives, they tap the delivery, take photos, note any issues, and mark it complete. Takes about 30 seconds.

Can vendors update delivery status themselves?

Yes. You can invite vendors as limited users who can only view and update their own deliveries. They mark items as "in transit" when trucks leave, and you get notified.

What happens if there's no cell service at the job site?

SiteProc works offline. Your team can document deliveries and take photos without connectivity. Data syncs automatically when they're back online.

How do I track deliveries across multiple projects?

The dashboard shows all deliveries across all projects. Filter by project, date range, vendor, or status. You can also set up alerts for specific projects only.

Can I integrate with my existing scheduling tools?

Deliveries sync with SiteProc's project calendar. Enterprise plans include API access for custom integrations with scheduling software.

Is the photo proof legally admissible?

Photos include timestamps, GPS coordinates, and user identification‚Äîeverything needed for dispute resolution. Many of our customers have used SiteProc documentation to resolve supplier disputes.

How far back can I see delivery history?

All delivery records and photos are stored permanently. You can access delivery history from any project at any time.

Can I track partial deliveries?

Yes. When a delivery is partial, your team notes what arrived and what's pending. The delivery stays open until everything is received.

What alerts can I set up?

Delivery scheduled, in-transit, arrived, completed, late (configurable threshold), and issues flagged. Choose email, push notifications, or both.

How does vendor performance tracking work?

SiteProc automatically tracks on-time delivery percentage, damage/issue frequency, and quantity accuracy for each vendor. Use this data when negotiating contracts or choosing suppliers.

Can I export delivery reports?

Yes. Export delivery history to PDF or CSV. Reports include all delivery details, photos, and receiver signatures.

Is training required for field teams?

No. The interface is intuitive‚Äîmost field workers figure it out in 5 minutes. We provide video tutorials and quick-start guides for anyone who needs them.
