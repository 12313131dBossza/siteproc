How We Evaluated Construction Software

Choosing the right construction company software is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your business. The wrong choice means wasted money, frustrated teams, and months of implementation headaches.

We evaluated each platform based on:

  1. Core Features: Project management, estimating, accounting integration, scheduling

Buildertrend excels in the residential construction space. Its client-facing features‚Äîselection management, progress photos, communication tools‚Äîare designed for homeowners who want to stay involved in their build.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which construction software is best for small contractors?

For contractors under $10M revenue, SiteProc or CoConstruct are the best options. SiteProc offers more features at a lower price point, while CoConstruct is more focused on custom home building. Avoid Procore unless you have the budget and staff for enterprise software.

How much does construction software typically cost?

Costs range from $50/month for basic tools to $10,000+/year for enterprise solutions. Per-user pricing (like SiteProc at $49-149/user/month) is most cost-effective for growing teams. Flat-fee pricing (like Buildertrend at $499-999/month) works better for large teams on many projects.

Can I switch from spreadsheets to construction software easily?

Yes, with the right software. Modern platforms like SiteProc are designed for quick implementation‚Äîmost teams are productive within 1-2 weeks. Enterprise platforms like Procore require more significant change management and often need dedicated administrators.

Do I need separate accounting software?

Yes, construction management software typically handles project operations while accounting software handles books. The key is integration. SiteProc includes free Zoho Books sync. Other platforms charge extra for QuickBooks or Xero integration.

What's the difference between project management and construction management software?

General project management tools (Monday.com, Asana, etc.) lack construction-specific features like change orders, submittals, RFIs, delivery tracking, and job costing. Construction management software is purpose-built for construction workflows.

Is Procore worth the cost?

For large GCs doing $100M+ revenue with complex projects and multiple office locations, yes. For smaller contractors, the cost and complexity don't justify the investment. SiteProc offers 80% of Procore's functionality at 20% of the price.

What construction software works best on mobile?

SiteProc was built mobile-first and works well on any device. Procore and Buildertrend have good mobile apps but were designed primarily for desktop use. If your team works primarily in the field, prioritize mobile experience when evaluating software.

Can construction software help with estimating?

Some platforms include estimating (Buildertrend, CoConstruct). Others focus on project execution after the estimate is won. If estimating is critical, evaluate this feature specifically. Dedicated estimating software like Bluebeam or PlanSwift may be better for complex takeoffs.

How long does implementation take?

For SiteProc, CoConstruct, or Jobber: 1-2 weeks to be productive. For Buildertrend: 2-4 weeks with good training. For Procore: 2-3 months with dedicated implementation support. Factor in implementation time and cost when comparing options.

Should I use construction-specific software or a general tool like Monday.com?

General tools are cheaper but lack construction-specific features. You'll spend hours building custom workflows that construction software handles out of the box. For serious construction operations, purpose-built software pays for itself in efficiency gains.

What if my team resists using new software?

Choose software with excellent mobile experience and minimal learning curve. Involve key team members in the evaluation process. Start with one or two features (like expense tracking) before rolling out everything. SiteProc is known for high adoption rates because of its simple interface.

Can I try construction software before buying?

Most platforms offer free trials. SiteProc offers 14 days free with full features. Buildertrend and CoConstruct offer demos. Procore typically requires sales calls before trials. Always test with real data and real team members before committing.
